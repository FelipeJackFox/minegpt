# Properties fields

Emit only the fields whose value is real and documented in the article.
Omit the entire line for any field that does not apply, is undocumented,
or has no meaningful value.

Type accepts only one of: Passive, Hostile, Neutral, Boss, Tameable, or
Rideable. Health is a number. Damage is a number, and the line is
omitted entirely if the mob does not attack. Walking speed is a decimal.
Swimming speed is a decimal and the line is omitted if the mob is not
aquatic-capable. Flying speed is a decimal and the line is omitted if
the mob does not fly. Spawn is a comma-separated list of biomes or
structures. Spawn light level is a number from 0 to 15 or the word
`any`, and the line is omitted if not documented. XP drop is a number
or hyphen-separated range, and the line is omitted if no specific
number is stated in the article (do not infer ranges from `yields
experience`). Tameable, Rideable, and Breedable each accept only the
value `Yes`; if the mob is not tameable, not rideable, or not breedable,
omit the entire line. Never write `Tameable: No`, `Rideable: No`, or
`Breedable: No`.

# Details focus

Inside the Details section, describe in prose: AI behavior (how the mob
pursues, what scares it, breeding mechanics, idle behavior); combat
patterns (attack interval, projectiles, special abilities) when
applicable; drops (regular drops, rare drops, Looting modifier effects,
with prose describing drop conditions); variants (color, biome, baby
form); and interactions with other mobs and players. All of this is
prose. There are no bullet points, no sub-headers, no bold inside the
Details paragraphs.

# Lens-specific focus

When the Current lens is a secondary lens (an `also_in`), emphasize the
matching aspect inside Details. For Tameable_mobs, emphasize the taming
mechanic, the required item, post-tame behavior, and commands like sit
or follow. For Bosses, emphasize phases, summoning ritual, unique
drops, achievement unlocked, and music change. For Aquatic_mobs,
emphasize water behavior, out-of-water consequences, and underwater
breeding. For Undead_mobs, emphasize Smite enchantment effect, healing
from harming potions, and day/night burning. For Nether_mobs, emphasize
Nether-only spawn, lava immunity, and piglin/hoglin interactions.

# Anti-hallucination

The article text is the only source of truth. Do not add facts from
your training data, even if you "know" them. If a stat or behavior is
not in the article, omit it; do not infer, approximate, or invent. Do
not invent percentages, drop chances, biomes, or numeric stats. If
unsure whether a behavior applies, leave it out.

# Output format anchor

Match this format exactly. Plain markdown headers. Field lines are

# Camel
## Overview
A camel is a large passive mob found in deserts and desert villages. It can be saddled and ridden by up to two players at once.

## Properties
Type: Passive
Health: 32
Walking speed: 3.885
Spawn: Desert village, Desert
XP drop: 1-3
Tameable: Yes
Rideable: Yes
Breedable: Yes

## Details
Camels wander aimlessly, avoiding hazards and obstacles, and occasionally sit for two to seven seconds before standing again. They flee instantly when harmed even while sitting. A sitting camel cannot be pushed by players or mobs, and will not stand if solid blocks are above it because doing so would inflict suffocation damage. Camels can step up 1.5 blocks high. They heal 2 HP per cactus consumed and regenerate 1 HP every 900 game ticks. Adult camels drop 1 to 3 experience when killed by a player.

## Obtaining
Camels spawn naturally in desert villages, with one camel per village during world generation. They can also be bred in captivity using cactus.
